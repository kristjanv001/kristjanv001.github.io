Short Story

Pablo Ruiz Picasso was a Spanish artist. He is regarded as one of the most influential artists of the 20th century and he is known for co-founding the Cubist movement. Some of his most famous works include the Les Demoiselles d'Avignon (1907), and Guernica (1937).

In the early years, Picasso painted in a naturalistic manner. During the first decade of the 20th century, he started to experiment with different techniques.

Picasso's work is often categorized into periods. These are: the Blue Period (1901–1904), the Rose Period (1904–1906), the African-influenced Period (1907–1909), Analytic Cubism (1909–1912), and Synthetic Cubism (1912–1919). His work of the late 1910s and early 1920s is in a neoclassical style, and his work in the mid-1920s often has characteristics of Surrealism.

Picasso is regarded one of the best-known figures in 20th-century art
